# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Three equally weighted roles share the same console:

- **Dealers** (admin + exec): logging activations daily, checking rebate eligibility and incentive totals. They are on mobile (PWA/Android) in a dealer shop, often mid-transaction. Speed and clarity beat completeness.
- **Owner / Accountant**: reconciliation, PDF report generation, auditing stock variances, approving large purchases. They are at a desk, need financial precision, and are the most trust-critical audience.
- **Sales Officers**: stock-in operations, CR transfers, activation entry. Field workers who need the form to get out of their way.

All three operate in Pakistan's OPPO dealer distribution network.

## Product Purpose

A multi-tenant SaaS console for OPPO phone dealers in Pakistan. It tracks every unit from purchase through activation, enforces incentive and rebate policies, catches CR discrepancies, and produces auditable PDF statements. Success means zero ambiguity about stock position and incentive entitlement for every dealer, every day.

## Brand Personality

Precise · Trusted · Efficient

The console speaks with the confidence of an accountant who has done this a thousand times. No hedging language, no decorative flair that doesn't carry data. Quiet authority.

## Anti-references

- Consumer mobile apps (bright gradients, big hero numbers, gamification energy)
- Decorative SaaS UIs that use color as wallpaper rather than signal
- Dashboards that lead with marketing metrics (big KPI tiles, hero stats for their own sake)
- Any interface where the styling draws more attention than the data it contains

## Design Principles

1. **Accuracy is the interface.** The numbers are the product. Every visual choice should make the right number easier to find and trust — not easier to admire.
2. **Operator confidence.** The UI should feel like it already knows what it's doing. No ambiguous states, no orphan loading spinners, no uncertain empty views. Every screen communicates status.
3. **One system, three roles.** Dealers, owners, and SOs share one visual language. Role-specific surfaces are scoped by access, not by a different design system.
4. **Mobile-first, keyboard-capable.** Primary use is PWA/Android in the field. Desktop power users (accountants, owners) benefit from dense tables and keyboard-accessible flows.
5. **Trust through legibility.** Typography hierarchy is non-negotiable. If two numbers look the same weight, that is a bug.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. 4.5:1 minimum for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components. Full keyboard navigation. Visible focus indicators. Reduced motion honored. No color-only encoding of financial status (always pair color with icon or label).
